From corporate brochure to industrial command centre.

01 — AuditWhat's wrong with the current site

Outdated visuals

  • Generic stock vibe — round red icons, dotted blob shapes, soft drop-shadows; reads like a SaaS template, not heavy industry.
  • Floating chat-bot, four call buttons, snapping CTAs all crowd the right rail and damage credibility.
  • "Get a Quote" diagonal pill is the only confident piece of design — and it gets lost.
  • Inconsistent spacing & type sizing. No clear scale system; CTAs vary across sections.

Hidden businesses

  • PEB is the visual landing slide but has no top-level nav home.
  • Mining is invisible — no nav slot, no homepage section, no landing page.
  • Grinding Media Balls — your newest and most strategic division — appears nowhere.
  • Trailers, Tankers, Chemicals, Turnkey live inside a "Products" mega-menu that nobody opens.

Missing pages / sections

  • Dedicated landing pages for Mining, Grinding Media, PEB sub-applications.
  • Project / Case-study library (you have the projects — show them).
  • Spec sheets & downloadable PDFs per product family.
  • Real lead capture beyond a single "Get in Touch" button.
  • News / Plant updates (Grinding Media plant launch — major news, missing).

Weak content

  • "Get quality iron and steel" — vague, generic, replaceable with any competitor.
  • Mission/Vision/Values are corporate-speak. Real differentiators (Made in Ghana, vertically integrated, owned logistics, new media plant) are buried.
  • No tonnages, no specs, no proof points beyond "Certified & Award Winner".

04 — Design DirectionThe system

Color palette

B5 Red#C8102E
Royal Blue#1E3A8A
Ink#0A0A0B
Paper#F6F4EF
White#FFFFFF

Red is the only action color. Blue is reserved for editorial blocks and certifications. Ink is the dominant industrial surface; paper is the editorial counterweight.

Typography

  • Inter Tight — UI, body, buttons, eyebrows. Tight tracking, modern, neutral.
  • Fraunces (italic) — accent words inside headlines. Adds editorial confidence.
  • JetBrains Mono — figure callouts, eyebrows, specs, tags. Reads as technical drawing.

Imagery style

  • Plant photography — molten steel, rolling mill, hot-end, finishing. Dark, dramatic, low-key.
  • Product photography — neutral grey, top-down or three-quarter, with mono callouts.
  • Site photography — wide drone shots of completed PEB structures.
  • No stock photos. No clip art. No emoji.

Layout principles

  • Editorial, not corporate. Large type. Generous negative space.
  • Tabular grids over decorative cards. Borders over shadows.
  • One accent per surface. Restraint over decoration.
  • Numbered indices everywhere — 01 / 02 / 03 — communicates rigour.

Mobile direction

  • Hamburger nav with full-bleed dark drawer.
  • Sticky bottom bar with WhatsApp + Phone + Quote.
  • Hero collapses to single column; stats stack 2x2.
  • Grids collapse to single column at 640px; 2-col at 1024px.

05 — Lead CaptureHow leads come in

Three layers — pick the most accessible to the buyer:

  1. Sticky floating cluster (bottom-right): WhatsApp, Phone, Email — present on every page.
  2. Header CTA: "Request a Quote" — leads to a single form with a division picker.
  3. Per-division CTA bands at the bottom of each landing page — pre-filled with division context.
Every division has its own email alias (steel@, peb@, mining@, grinding@) so leads route directly to the right desk without a triage step.

07 — Developer NotesImplementation

  • Static HTML reference build provided in this project — see index.html, pages/peb.html, pages/mining.html, pages/grinding-media.html, pages/steel.html, pages/contact.html.
  • Shared design system in assets/styles.css.
  • Header & footer partials in assets/header.html and assets/footer.html; included via assets/include.js.
  • Recommended CMS: WordPress (with ACF) or Webflow — simple enough for marketing edits, structured enough for a 30-page site.
  • Form handling: pipe to HubSpot or Zoho with division-routed notifications.
  • Performance budget: <200KB JS, lazy-loaded photography, served via CDN. Aim for LCP < 2.0s.
  • Add structured data — Organization, Product, BreadcrumbList — for rich results.
  • WhatsApp click-to-chat link present on every page; phone tel: links on every page.
